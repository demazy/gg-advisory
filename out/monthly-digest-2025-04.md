**Top Items**

- **Record-Breaking Year for Renewables**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-04-17
  - **Summary:** In 2024, global renewable energy capacity surged by 585 GW, marking a 15.1% annual growth rate, surpassing the previous year's 14.3%. Renewables now represent 46.4% of total installed power capacity, with solar and wind contributing 96.6% of this expansion. The levelized cost of electricity from renewables continues to decline, reinforcing their economic viability. 
  - *Why it matters*  
    - This growth highlights investment opportunities for AU/EU businesses in renewable sectors.  
    - The trend supports compliance with international climate targets, enhancing market competitiveness.
  - **Signals to watch**  
    - Continued monitoring of renewable capacity growth rates and investment trends.  
    - Potential policy changes influencing renewable energy financing.
  - **Sources**

- **Importance of Early-Stage Financing**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-04-03
  - **Summary:** Early-stage financing is crucial for the success of renewable energy projects, which require substantial upfront investments for feasibility studies and risk assessments. Many projects, particularly in developing regions like Sub-Saharan Africa, struggle to secure necessary funding, leading to delays in achieving financial closure. The Energy Transition Accelerator Financing (ETAF) platform aims to address these funding gaps.
  - *Why it matters*  
    - Access to early-stage financing can accelerate project development, benefiting AU/EU investors.  
    - Addressing funding disparities can enhance global energy transition efforts and compliance with climate goals.
  - **Signals to watch**  
    - Developments in funding mechanisms for early-stage renewable projects.  
    - Trends in project timelines and financial closure rates.
  - **Sources**  
    - https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2025/Apr/The-Success-of-Global-Energy-Transitions-Starts-with-Early-Stage-Project-Financing
