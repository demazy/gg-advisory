# KPI Watch — 2025-10-20

## 1. Media Assets
**Summary:**  
The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) is enhancing its media assets by providing comprehensive access to content from its Annual Forum 2024. This includes media releases, key speeches, photos, and video highlights. The forum aims to address critical topics such as the state of the economy and access to financial services, ensuring that stakeholders are informed about regulatory developments. Journalists can access this information to enhance their reporting and provide accurate updates to the public.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Increased access to real-time media content from ASIC’s events, allowing for timely reporting.
- Streamlined communication with ASIC's media unit for inquiries and interview requests.

*Data & controls:*
- Journalists must register upon visiting ASIC premises, ensuring controlled access to sensitive information.
- Requests for comments and interviews will require detailed outlines, enhancing the quality of information shared.

**Sources:** [ASIC Media Assets](https://asic.gov.au/newsroom-beta/media-assets)

## 2. Contact ASIC Media Unit
**Summary:**  
ASIC has established a dedicated media unit to facilitate communication with journalists. This unit assists with inquiries, interview requests, and information about visiting ASIC premises. By providing clear guidelines on how to contact them, ASIC aims to foster better relationships with the media, ensuring that accurate information is disseminated to the public. This initiative is crucial for maintaining transparency and accountability in financial regulation.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Journalists can now easily access contact information for the ASIC media unit, improving response times for inquiries.
- Clear guidelines on submitting requests for interviews and comments streamline the process for obtaining information.

*Data & controls:*
- Journalists must provide detailed information about their requests, ensuring that ASIC can prepare adequately for inquiries.
- All media representatives must sign in upon visiting ASIC, maintaining security and control over the information shared.

**Tags:** #ASIC #MediaUnit #Journalism  
**Sources:** [Contact ASIC Media Unit](https://asic.gov.au/about-asic/news-centre/contact-asic-media-unit/)

## 3. Bannings and Alerts
**Summary:**  
ASIC's "Bannings and Alerts" section serves as a critical resource for journalists and the public, providing updates on regulatory actions taken against individuals or entities. This feature enhances transparency in the financial sector by informing stakeholders about compliance issues and regulatory enforcement. By regularly updating this section, ASIC ensures that the media and the public are aware of significant developments that may impact the financial landscape.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Journalists can access up-to-date information on regulatory actions, allowing for informed reporting on compliance issues.
- The alerts serve as a resource for developing stories related to financial misconduct and regulatory enforcement.

*Data & controls:*
- Regular updates ensure that the information remains current, requiring reporters to check frequently for new alerts.
- Journalists may need to verify details with ASIC before publishing to ensure accuracy.

**Tags:** #ASIC #Bannings #RegulatoryAlerts  
**Sources:** [Bannings and Alerts](https://asic.gov.au/newsroom/bannings-and-alerts/)
