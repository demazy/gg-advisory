### Iberian Blackout Raises Concerns
- **SECTION:** Energy Transition
- **PUBLISHED:** 2025-05-28
- **Summary:** A major blackout on the Iberian Peninsula on 28 April 2025 affected 55 million people, prompting an investigation by the European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity (ENSTO-E). The blackout, which occurred during business hours, was not an isolated incident, as similar events have been reported in Chile and Sri Lanka earlier in 2025. The swift restoration of power within 12-16 hours underscores the importance of robust emergency protocols. 
- *Why it matters:*
  - Highlights the potential for similar incidents in Australia’s National Electricity Market (NEM).
  - Urges Australian utilities to enhance grid resilience and emergency preparedness.
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Ongoing investigations may lead to new regulatory measures in the EU.
- **Sources:**
  - [Energy Networks Australia](https://www.energynetworks.com.au/news/energy-insider/2025-energy-insider/watt-happened-unravelling-the-iberian-power-mystery/)

### IRENA Calls for Renewable Projects in Central Asia
- **SECTION:** Sustainable Finance & Investment
- **PUBLISHED:** 2025-05-26
- **Summary:** The International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA) is inviting proposals for renewable energy projects in Central Asia, with a submission deadline of 15 August 2025. This initiative aims to enhance financing opportunities through the Climate Investment Platform (CIP) and the Energy Transition Accelerator Financing (ETAF) platform, supporting countries in diversifying their energy mix and achieving climate goals.
- *Why it matters:*
  - Opens avenues for Australian investors to engage in emerging markets.
  - Aligns with global efforts to meet renewable energy targets and climate commitments.
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Proposal submissions due by 15 August 2025.
- **Sources:**
  - [IRENA News](https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2025/May/IRENA-Issues-Call-for-Renewable-Energy-Projects-in-Central-Asia)

### Closing the Financing Gap in the Global South
- **SECTION:** Sustainable Finance & Investment
- **PUBLISHED:** 2025-05-08
- **Summary:** IRENA emphasizes the need for USD 31.5 trillion in investments to meet renewable energy capacity targets by 2030. Currently, only 10% of global renewable energy investments reach developing economies, which face higher risks. IRENA calls for enhanced international cooperation and strategic deployment of limited public funds to create bankable projects and clear regulatory frameworks.
- *Why it matters:*
  - Highlights investment opportunities for Australian firms in developing regions.
  - Encourages policy reforms that could benefit Australian investors looking to enter emerging markets.
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Ongoing discussions on international cooperation and investment strategies.
- **Sources:**
  - [IRENA News](https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2025/May/Closing-the-Financing-Gap-in-the-Global-South-Requires-a-Multi-Pronged-Approach)

### Youth Capacity Building Through Peer Learning
- **SECTION:** Sustainable Finance & Investment
- **PUBLISHED:** 2025-05-20
- **Summary:** The I ACT initiative by IRENA aims to empower youth in climate change education through peer-to-peer learning. It includes a toolkit for training peer educators and has trained 161 young leaders across 42 countries. This initiative responds to the urgent need for climate literacy among younger generations, equipping them to address climate challenges.
- *Why it matters:*
  - Fosters a new generation of climate leaders, crucial for future sustainability efforts.
  - Aligns with Australia’s educational and climate goals, potentially influencing local programs.
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Continued implementation of workshops and training sessions globally.
- **Sources:**
  - [IRENA News](https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2025/May/I-ACT-Building-Youths-Capacity-Through-Peer-to-Peer-Learning)

### Insights from US Energy Infrastructure
- **SECTION:** Energy Transition
- **PUBLISHED:** 2025-05-12
- **Summary:** Observations from a recent trip to the US highlight challenges in low-voltage electrical infrastructure, particularly in California. The US operates at 120 volts, necessitating smaller transformers that may struggle with the demands of a rapidly changing energy landscape. These insights could inform Australian energy infrastructure development and policy.
- *Why it matters:*
  - Offers lessons for Australian utilities in managing infrastructure challenges.
  - Encourages proactive measures to adapt to evolving energy demands.
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Potential policy shifts in response to infrastructure challenges.
