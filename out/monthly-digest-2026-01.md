- **Showcasing how companies can turn ESG data into decision-useful insights**
  - **SECTION:** ESG Reporting
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-28
  - **Summary:** The GRI has launched a series titled 'ESG Reporting in Action' that features case studies demonstrating how companies utilize digital tools to manage sustainability data. The first episode highlights PlanetWise and the Climate and Conservation Consortium, showcasing how automation enhances carbon data insights. This initiative aims to simplify reporting processes and improve data quality across various sectors.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Enhances the ability of companies to report accurately, which is crucial for compliance with evolving regulations.
    - Provides a framework for businesses to learn from peers, potentially reducing reporting errors.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Upcoming episodes will continue to provide insights throughout the year.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.globalreporting.org/news/news-center/showcasing-how-companies-can-turn-esg-data-into-decision-useful-insights/

- **Energy Networks Australia welcomes Solar Sharer Offer next steps**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-23
  - **Summary:** Energy Networks Australia (ENA) has endorsed the Solar Sharer Offer outcomes paper, emphasizing the need to bridge the gap between energy access for households with solar capabilities and those without. ENA aims to collaborate with the Australian Energy Regulator to ensure effective policy and infrastructure investment for the program's success.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Addresses energy equity, crucial for inclusive clean energy transition strategies.
    - Aligns with regulatory frameworks that may impact energy tariffs and infrastructure investments.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Review of design principles and alignment with market settings is ongoing.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.energynetworks.com.au/news/energy-networks-australia-welcomes-solar-sharer-offer-next-steps/

- **System security and reliability**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-23
  - **Summary:** The Australian Energy Market Operator (AEMO) is responsible for maintaining the security and reliability of the electricity grid, especially as renewable energy sources increase. The report outlines the technical challenges posed by renewables and emphasizes the need for innovative solutions to ensure grid stability.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Critical for businesses relying on stable energy supply as renewables become more prevalent.
    - Impacts regulatory compliance and operational strategies in energy-intensive sectors.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Ongoing research and funding for projects aimed at enhancing grid security.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://arena.gov.au/renewable-energy/system-security-reliability/

- **CEFC backs new QIC infrastructure investment to accelerate energy transition**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-20
  - **Summary:** The Clean Energy Finance Corporation (CEFC) has committed $70 million to the QIC Global Infrastructure Fund II to support decarbonisation in energy and transport sectors. This investment aims to lower emissions across the value chain, focusing on infrastructure that facilitates the energy transition.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Supports infrastructure development critical for achieving net zero emissions by 2050.
    - Provides investment opportunities in sectors undergoing significant transformation.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - The fund aims to establish greenhouse gas emission reduction targets validated by the Science Based Target Initiative.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.cefc.com.au/media/media-release/cefc-backs-new-qic-infrastructure-investment-to-accelerate-energy-transition/

- **Bioenergy / Energy from waste**
  - **SECTION:** Sustainable Finance & Investment
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-20
  - **Summary:** Bioenergy, derived from biomass, is a renewable energy source that can significantly contribute to Australia’s clean energy generation. The Australian Government's Bioenergy Roadmap aims to guide investment and policy decisions in this sector, which currently contributes 5% to Australia's energy mix.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Offers potential for waste reduction and energy generation, aligning with sustainability goals.
    - Encourages investment in rural economies and supports local energy initiatives.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Continued support for bioenergy projects and research to enhance commercial viability.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://arena.gov.au/renewable-energy/bioenergy/

- **Post-implementation Reviews**
  - **SECTION:** ESG Reporting

  - **Summary:** The IASB conducts post-implementation reviews (PIR) to assess the effectiveness of new IFRS standards.     - Ensures that financial reporting standards remain relevant and effective for businesses.
    - Impacts compliance and reporting practices across various sectors.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Stakeholder engagement and feedback mechanisms are ongoing, influencing future standards.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.ifrs.org/content/ifrs/home/projects/post-implementation-reviews.html
