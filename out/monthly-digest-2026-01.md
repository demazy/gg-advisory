## Energy Transition
- **Solar Sharer Offer Next Steps Announced**
  - PUBLISHED: 2026-01-23
  - Summary: Energy Networks Australia (ENA) has welcomed the outcomes paper for the Solar Sharer Offer, emphasizing the need to bridge the gap between energy "haves" and "have-nots." ENA CEO Dom van den Berg highlighted the importance of aligning policy, infrastructure investment, and energy tariffs for the program's success. The initiative aims to provide equitable access to clean energy solutions for all Australians, including renters.
  - Why it matters: This initiative is crucial for promoting energy equity in Australia's clean energy transition.
  
- **Australia’s Energy System Wish List for 2026**
  - PUBLISHED: 2025-12-18
  - Summary: ENA has outlined a practical wish list for Australia's energy system, focusing on better utilization of existing grids, efficient transmission planning, and recognizing the role of gas in the energy transition. The list emphasizes the importance of policy levers that can enhance system resilience and affordability for consumers.
  - Why it matters: Implementing these wishes could lead to significant cost savings and improved energy system performance.

## ESG Reporting
- **EFRAG Opens Call for Candidates for SR TEG**
  - PUBLISHED: 2026-01-27
  - Summary: EFRAG has opened applications for its Sustainability Reporting Technical Expert Group (SR TEG), inviting professionals with ESG expertise to apply by 1 March 2026. Candidates from diverse backgrounds are encouraged to contribute to the development of European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS).
  - Why it matters: This initiative aims to enhance the quality and consistency of sustainability reporting across Europe.

- **Deadline Extended for EFRAG FR TEG Applications**
  - PUBLISHED: 2026-01-12
  - Summary: EFRAG has extended the application deadline for its Financial Reporting Technical Expert Group to 31 January 2026. The group seeks professionals with diverse backgrounds to support European financial reporting efforts, with appointments starting on 1 April 2026.
  - Why it matters: The extension allows more candidates to participate in shaping financial reporting standards in Europe.

- **ISSB Update on Nature-Related Risks**
  - PUBLISHED: 2026-01-01
  - Summary: The ISSB met on 28 January 2026 to discuss its standard-setting project on nature-related risks and opportunities. The board agreed to proceed with developing disclosure requirements, marking a significant step in sustainability reporting.
  - Why it matters: This project is essential for integrating environmental considerations into financial reporting.
