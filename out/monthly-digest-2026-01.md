## Energy Transition
### Solar Sharer Offer Next Steps
- **PUBLISHER:** energynetworks.com.au
- **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-23
- **Summary:** Energy Networks Australia (ENA) has welcomed the release of the Solar Sharer Offer outcomes paper, which aims to address energy inequities in Australia’s clean energy transition. ENA CEO Dom van den Berg highlighted the need for policy alignment and infrastructure investment to ensure the program's success. The initiative seeks to provide benefits to households unable to install solar or electric vehicles, thereby closing the gap between energy "haves" and "have-nots."
- **Why it matters:** 
  - The program aims to enhance energy access for underserved communities.
  - It encourages collaboration with regulatory bodies to optimize energy tariffs and infrastructure.
- **Source:** https://www.energynetworks.com.au/news/energy-networks-australia-welcomes-solar-sharer-offer-next-steps/

### Quarterly Carbon Market Report Released
- **PUBLISHER:** cer.gov.au
- **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-21
- **Summary:** The Quarterly Carbon Market Report (QCMR) provides insights into Australia’s carbon markets, detailing supply and demand dynamics. The latest report, published on January 21, 2026, includes data on Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) project registrations and the performance of renewable energy schemes. It serves as a key resource for understanding market trends and opportunities.
- **Why it matters:** 
  - It offers critical data for stakeholders in the carbon market.
  - The report helps inform investment decisions in renewable energy.
- **Source:** https://www.cer.gov.au/markets/reports-and-data/quarterly-carbon-market-reports

- **PUBLISHER:** efrag.org
- **PUBLISHED:** 2026-01-27
- **Summary:** EFRAG has announced a call for applications to join its Sustainability Reporting Technical Expert Group (SR TEG). Applications are open until March 1, 2026, and candidates from diverse professional backgrounds are encouraged to apply.

  - It seeks to ensure diverse representation in the development of sustainability standards.
