## IFASS Meetings Foster Global Reporting Dialogue

The International Forum of Accounting Standard Setters (IFASS) serves as a platform for national accounting standard setters and organizations to discuss financial and sustainability reporting issues. Recent meetings have included participation from the IASB and ISSB, ensuring a collaborative approach to standard-setting. EFRAG's Chiara Del Prete chairs IFASS until March 2025, highlighting the ongoing commitment to enhancing global reporting practices.

*Why it matters:*

- Collaboration among standard setters can lead to more harmonized reporting frameworks, reducing compliance burdens.

## 1. Grid Adequacy
- **Source:** [IRENA News](https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2025/Oct/World-Breaks-Renewable-Records-but-Must-Move-Faster-to-Hit-2030-Tripling-Goal)
- **Summary:** The recent IRENA report highlights that while global renewable capacity additions reached a record 582 GW in 2024, significant bottlenecks in grid infrastructure are hindering further expansion. To meet the COP28 target of tripling renewable capacity by 2030, a staggering 1,122 GW must be added annually from 2025 onwards, necessitating urgent upgrades and expansions in grid infrastructure to accommodate this growth.

## 2. Resource Adequacy

## 1. Contact ASIC Media Unit
**Summary:**  
The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) has established a dedicated Media Unit to assist journalists with inquiries related to their operations. This unit facilitates communication by providing contact details, guidelines for visiting ASIC premises, and protocols for requesting interviews or comments. Journalists are encouraged to provide specific details about their inquiries to ensure a timely and accurate response. The Media Unit aims to uphold transparency and accessibility, although legal constraints may limit the information provided. This initiative underscores ASIC's commitment to engaging with the media effectively.

*What changes for reporters:*

- Only media representatives can contact the Media Unit, ensuring focused communication.

*Data & controls:*

- All inquiries are logged to maintain a record of media interactions.

**Tags:** ASIC, Media Relations, Journalism  
**Sources:** [ASIC Media Unit](https://asic.gov.au/about-asic/news-centre/contact-asic-media-unit/)

---

## 2. TCFD Report Archive Disbandment
**Summary:**  
As of November 2023, the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) has disbanded following the release of its 2023 status report. The Financial Stability Board (FSB) has tasked the IFRS Foundation with overseeing climate-related disclosures moving forward. The TCFD report archive will remain accessible as a resource, but no further updates will be made. This transition marks a significant shift in the landscape of climate-related reporting, emphasizing the need for companies to adapt to new oversight mechanisms and maintain compliance with evolving disclosure standards.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Reporters must familiarize themselves with the IFRS Foundation's new guidelines for climate-related disclosures.
- Companies may need to adjust their reporting frameworks to align with the new oversight structure.

*Data & controls:*
- Companies should ensure their climate-related disclosures meet the IFRS Foundation's standards.
- Ongoing monitoring of compliance will be essential as the new framework is implemented.

**Tags:** TCFD, Climate Reporting, IFRS  
**Sources:** [TCFD Report Archive](https://www.fsb-tcfd.org/report-archive/)
