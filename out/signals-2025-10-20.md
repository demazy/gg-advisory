## EFRAG Endorsement Status Update

**Summary:**  
On September 30, 2025, EFRAG released its latest endorsement status report, detailing the ARC's written procedure vote on IFRS 18 regarding presentation and disclosure in financial statements. Additionally, EFRAG has recommended IFRS 19, which pertains to subsidiaries without public accountability, for endorsement in the EU. Stakeholders are invited to comment on the draft endorsement advice for amendments to this standard by November 28, 2025.

*Why it matters:*  
- Businesses must prepare for compliance with new IFRS standards, impacting financial reporting practices.  
- Timely engagement in the consultation process can influence the final endorsement decisions.

*Signals to watch:*  
- **November 28, 2025:** Deadline for stakeholder comments on draft endorsement advice.  
- **October 14, 2025:** EFRAG joint webcast meetings discussing financial reporting updates.

## Strategic Signals

# Strategic Signals — 2025-10-20

## Grid Adequacy
- **Market Signal**: The increasing integration of renewable energy sources is necessitating advancements in grid infrastructure to ensure reliability and stability. 
- **Source**: [Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA)](https://arena.gov.au/news/)
- **Insight**: As more intermittent resources like solar and wind come online, grid operators must enhance their capabilities to manage fluctuations in supply and demand effectively.

## Resource Adequacy
- **Market Signal**: There is a growing emphasis on resource adequacy, particularly in the context of energy storage solutions such as batteries and pumped hydro.
- **Source**: [ARENA News](https://arena.gov.au/news)
- **Insight**: The need for reliable backup resources is critical as the share of renewables increases, ensuring that energy supply meets demand even during low generation periods.

## Project Pipelines
- **Market Signal**: Significant investments are being made in project pipelines for renewable energy technologies, including solar, wind, and hydrogen projects.
- **Source**: [ARENA News](https://arena.gov.au/news/page/2/)
- **Insight**: The expansion of project pipelines indicates a robust commitment to transitioning towards a sustainable energy future, with a focus on scaling up capacity and diversifying energy sources.

## Market Risks
- **Market Signal**: The transition to renewable energy is accompanied by various market risks, including regulatory changes, technological advancements, and financial uncertainties.
- **Source**: [ARENA News](https://arena.gov.au/news/)
- **Insight**: Stakeholders must navigate these risks carefully to ensure that investments in renewable energy projects remain viable and attractive, particularly in a rapidly evolving market landscape.

### Summary
The energy transition landscape is characterized by a pressing need for grid and resource adequacy, a robust project pipeline, and an awareness of market risks. Stakeholders must strategically align their efforts to address these signals to ensure a successful transition to a sustainable energy future.

## 1. EFRAG FRB & EFRAG SRB Joint Hybrid Meeting
**Summary:**  
On October 14, 2025, the European Financial Reporting Advisory Group (EFRAG) will host a joint hybrid meeting between the Financial Reporting Board (FRB) and the Sustainability Reporting Board (SRB). This meeting is designed for registered observers and will take place from 13:00 to 15:00 CET. The agenda and supporting documents will be accessible online, and participants can register to watch the live webcast or access an on-demand recording. The meeting may include closed sessions, ensuring focused discussions on critical topics in financial and sustainability reporting.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Increased transparency in sustainability reporting practices discussed during the meeting.
- Potential updates to reporting standards that may affect future disclosures.

*Data & controls:*
- Access to meeting agendas and documents will be crucial for accurate reporting.
- Observers must register to ensure compliance with participation protocols.

**Tags:** EFRAG, Sustainability Reporting, Financial Reporting  
**Sources:** [EFRAG News](https://www.efrag.org/en/news-and-calendar/news/efrag-frb-efrag-srb-joint-hybrid-meeting-14-october-2025)

## 2. EFRAG FRB & EFRAG FR TEG Joint Webcast Meeting
**Summary:**  
Also on October 14, 2025, EFRAG will conduct a joint webcast meeting between the Financial Reporting Board (FRB) and the Financial Reporting Technical Expert Group (FR TEG) from 09:00 to 10:30 CET. This meeting is open to duly registered observers and aims to discuss key developments in financial reporting standards. Participants can register to view the live webcast or access the recording later. As with other EFRAG meetings, closed sessions may occur, emphasizing the importance of confidentiality in certain discussions.

*What changes for reporters:*
- New insights into financial reporting standards that may influence future reporting practices.
- Opportunities to engage with experts on emerging financial reporting issues.

*Data & controls:*
- Observers will need to ensure they have the latest documents and agenda for accurate reporting.
- Registration is mandatory to access the meeting content.

**Tags:** EFRAG, Financial Reporting, Webcast  
**Sources:** [EFRAG News](https://www.efrag.org/en/news-and-calendar/news/efrag-frb-efrag-fr-teg-joint-webcast-meeting-14-october-2025)
