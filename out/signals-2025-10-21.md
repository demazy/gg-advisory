## 1. Contact ASIC Media Unit
**Summary:**  
The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) has established a dedicated Media Unit to assist journalists with inquiries regarding financial regulations and corporate governance. This unit facilitates access to information, interviews, and speakers from ASIC. Journalists are required to provide specific details about their inquiries, including their media outlet, intended questions, and deadlines. The unit aims to respond promptly while adhering to legal and policy constraints.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Reporters must now provide detailed inquiries to receive timely responses from ASIC.
- Access to ASIC premises for interviews will be accompanied by a Media Unit representative.

*Data & controls:*
- Journalists can contact the Media Unit via phone or email for inquiries.
- ASIC's media policy emphasizes accurate and thorough responses, with limitations based on legal constraints.

**Tags:** ASIC, Media, Journalists, Inquiries  
**Sources:** [ASIC Media Unit](https://asic.gov.au/about-asic/news-centre/contact-asic-media-unit/)

---

## 2. TCFD Report Archive Disbandment
**Summary:**  
As of November 2023, the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) has disbanded following the release of its 2023 status report. The Financial Stability Board (FSB) has delegated the monitoring of climate-related disclosures to the IFRS Foundation. The TCFD's website will remain available as a resource, but it will no longer be updated. This transition marks a significant shift in how companies will report on climate-related risks and opportunities moving forward.

*What changes for reporters:*
- Reporters will need to refer to the IFRS Foundation for updates on climate-related disclosures.
- The TCFD's recommendations will still be relevant, but companies may adopt new guidelines from the IFRS Foundation.

*Data & controls:*
- Companies are expected to continue using TCFD recommendations until new IFRS guidelines are established.
- The archived materials will serve as a reference for best practices in climate-related disclosures.

**Tags:** TCFD, Climate Disclosures, IFRS, Reporting  
**Sources:** [TCFD Report Archive](https://www.fsb-tcfd.org/report-archive/)
