- **Renewable Power Generation Costs in 2024**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-07-22
  - **Summary:** The infographic on renewable power generation costs in 2024 highlights that renewables have maintained their price advantage over fossil fuels, saving the electricity sector USD467 billion in fuel costs. In 2024, there was a net increase of 582 GW in global renewable generation capacity, marking a 15.1% growth from 2023. The report emphasizes the need to triple renewable capacity by 2030 to ensure energy security and market resilience.
  - *Why it matters* 
    - Australian and European businesses can leverage the cost advantages of renewables to enhance competitiveness.
    - Investors should consider the growing market for renewable energy as a stable investment opportunity.
  - **Signals to watch** 
    - The push to triple renewable capacity by 2030.

- **What is NEPKI – and how can you help shape its future?**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-07-10
  - **Summary:** The National Energy Public Key Infrastructure (NEPKI) initiative aims to secure Australia’s clean energy future by enabling secure digital communication between energy networks and consumer energy resources. NEPKI is part of the National Consumer Energy Resources Roadmap and will facilitate the integration of smart devices into the grid. It is being established as a not-for-profit organization with a diverse membership model to enhance trust and interoperability in the energy sector.
  - *Why it matters* 
    - This initiative is crucial for Australian businesses to ensure compliance with evolving energy security standards.
    - It opens opportunities for technology providers and OEMs in the energy sector.
  - **Signals to watch** 
    - Final determination from the ACCC expected in July.
  - **Sources** 
    - https://www.energynetworks.com.au/news/energy-insider/2025-energy-insider/what-is-nepki-and-how-can-you-help-shape-its-future/

- **How Decentralised Renewables Transform Healthcare Services in Rural Zimbabwe**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-07-02
  - **Summary:** Decentralised renewable energy (DRE) has significantly improved healthcare services in rural Zimbabwe, supporting over 180,000 deliveries annually. Many health facilities previously struggled with unreliable electricity, impacting patient care. DRE has provided 55% of new electricity connections in Sub-Saharan Africa from 2020 to 2022, demonstrating its potential to enhance healthcare delivery in underserved areas.
  - *Why it matters* 
    - Australian and European investors can explore opportunities in DRE projects that improve healthcare access.
    - This model can inform similar initiatives in regions facing energy access challenges.
  - **Signals to watch** 
    - The ongoing expansion of DRE solutions in Sub-Saharan Africa.
  - **Sources** 
    - https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2021/Jul/How-Decentralised-Renewables-Transform-Healthcare-Services-in-Rural-Zimbabwe
