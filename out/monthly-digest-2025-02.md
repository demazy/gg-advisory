- **Tripling off-grid renewables is a catalyst for sustainable development in rural communities**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-02-24
  - **Summary:** The COP28 UAE consensus has committed to tripling installed renewable energy capacity to 11.2 terawatts and doubling energy efficiency improvements by 2030. Off-grid renewables are vital for enhancing energy access, particularly in rural and remote areas, where 83% of the global electricity access deficit now resides. From 2014 to 2022, the number of people without electricity decreased from 1 billion to 685 million, but progress has stagnated since 2018. Off-grid solutions like solar home systems and mini-grids have provided electricity to 155 million people in 2023, fostering socio-economic development and supporting multiple Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
  - *Why it matters*
    - This initiative can drive investment opportunities in renewable technologies for AU/EU businesses.
    - Compliance with global energy targets may require adaptation of local policies and practices.
  - **Signals to watch**
    - Monitor the implementation of the COP28 commitments and related funding opportunities.
