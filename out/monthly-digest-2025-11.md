- **Five Takeaways from COP30 in Belém**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-11-24
  - **Summary:** At COP30, IRENA emphasized the need to triple renewable energy capacity and double energy efficiency by 2030. The report highlighted that 11.2 TW of installed renewable power capacity is achievable, but investment bottlenecks remain. Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) are crucial for guiding the transition. IRENA has supported 102 countries in developing climate commitments since 2020, impacting 6 billion people. The focus on implementation and infrastructure was underscored as essential for a just energy transition.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Aligning with NDCs can enhance compliance for EU businesses.
    - Investment in infrastructure is critical for Australian energy companies.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Upcoming deadlines for national renewable targets.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.irena.org/News/articles/2025/Nov/Five-Takeaways-from-COP30-in-Belem

- **Global Renewable Energy Investment Hit USD 807 Billion in 2024**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-11-17
  - **Summary:** Global investments in renewable energy reached USD 807 billion in 2024, accounting for one-third of the total USD 2.4 trillion energy transition investment. Despite this record, growth slowed to 7.3% from 32% the previous year. Solar PV investments surged to USD 554 billion, but 90% of funding remains concentrated in advanced economies, highlighting the need for inclusive financing strategies.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Investors should consider diversifying into emerging markets for growth.
    - Australian firms may face competitive pressures from advanced economies.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - Monitoring investment trends in emerging markets.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2025/Nov/Global-Renewable-Energy-Investment-Hit-USD-807-Billion-in-2024

- **Global utilities set out USD 1 trillion investment plans at COP30 as grid spend grows**
  - **SECTION:** Energy Transition
  - **PUBLISHED:** 2025-11-14
  - **Summary:** At COP30, members of the Utilities for Net Zero Alliance announced plans to increase annual energy transition investments to USD 148 billion, focusing heavily on grid infrastructure. This commitment will mobilize over USD 1 trillion by 2030, with a significant portion dedicated to enhancing grid capabilities and renewable energy capacity.
  - *Why it matters:*
    - Australian utilities can leverage similar investments to modernize infrastructure.
    - EU businesses may benefit from increased grid reliability and capacity.
  - **Signals to watch:**
    - New grid financing principles to be implemented.
  - **Sources:**
    - https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2025/Nov/Global-utilities-set-out-USD-1-trillion-investment-plans-at-COP30-as-grid-spend-grows
