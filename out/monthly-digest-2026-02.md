## Energy Transition

**Energy security: Keeping the lights on through the energy transition (QLD)**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-26
Summary: Rainer Korte, Commissioner at AEMC, discussed the ongoing energy transition in Australia, emphasising the need for a fivefold increase in grid-scale wind and solar by 2050. The Draft 2026 Integrated System Plan (ISP) indicates electricity consumption in the National Electricity Market (NEM) is expected to nearly double by 2050, necessitating significant investment. The estimated annualised capital cost for utility-scale generation, storage, transmission, and distribution infrastructure is $128 billion by 2050.
Why it matters: CFOs at energy-intensive companies should model exposure to rising electricity costs now — the ISP's $128B annual investment requirement signals sustained upward pressure on network charges and wholesale prices through the 2030s.
Sources: https://www.aemc.gov.au/news-centre/speeches/energy-security-keeping-lights-through-energy-transition-qld

**Energy security: Keeping the lights on through the energy transition (NSW)**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-24
Summary: In a parallel NSW address, Rainer Korte highlighted the critical need for maintaining system security as coal plants retire. The AEMO Transition Plan for System Security identifies gaps requiring coordinated action over the next decade. The retirement of Eraring Power Station has been extended to 30 April 2029, providing additional time for new infrastructure development.
Why it matters: Boards with operations dependent on baseload supply in NSW should review continuity plans against the 2029 Eraring retirement date and the AEMO security gap timeline.
Sources: https://www.aemc.gov.au/news-centre/speeches/energy-security-keeping-lights-through-energy-transition-nsw

**Carbon Abatement Schemes Deliver Emissions Reductions Comparable to a Year of Road Transport**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01
Summary: The Clean Energy Regulator reported that carbon abatement schemes reduced emissions by approximately 83.7 million tonnes CO2-e in 2025 — equivalent to removing all cars and trucks from Australian roads for a full year. Uptake of home batteries surged, with over 193,000 installations delivering 4.6 GWh of capacity under the Cheaper Home Batteries Program.
Why it matters: Corporates with large Scope 3 supply-chain emissions should note the scale of available ACCU supply; growing demand from Safeguard Mechanism facilities is expected to tighten the ACCU market in 2026–27.
Sources: https://www.cer.gov.au/news-and-media/media/2026/february/carbon-abatement-schemes-deliver-emissions-reductions-comparable-to-year-road-transport

## ESG Reporting

**ISSB Update February 2026**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01
Summary: The ISSB met on 25 February 2026 and made preliminary decisions on the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards. Proposed amendments to IFRS S2 industry-based guidance are expected to be published in March 2026, with an effective date 12–18 months post-issuance. The ISSB also confirmed it is enhancing SASB Standards to align with climate-related content and improving connectivity with IFRS Accounting Standards.
Why it matters: CFOs at entities already applying or preparing for IFRS S2 must track the March 2026 exposure draft — the 12–18 month implementation window means board sign-off on disclosure controls should begin now.
Sources: https://www.ifrs.org/content/ifrs/home/news-and-events/updates/issb/2026/issb-update-february-2026.html

**EFRAG Calls for Balanced and Cost-Effective Approach to Proposed GHG Protocol Scope 2 Changes**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-20
Summary: EFRAG submitted its position on proposed changes to the GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance, advocating for clarity and proportionality. EFRAG emphasised the need for a careful cost-benefit analysis and adequate consultation periods before finalising revisions that would alter how companies account for electricity-related emissions under both market-based and location-based methods.
Why it matters: Australian companies reporting under ISSB or preparing voluntary Scope 2 disclosures should monitor GHG Protocol outcomes — revised Scope 2 rules could require restating prior-year emissions figures and renegotiating PPAs.
Sources: https://www.efrag.org/en/news-and-calendar/news/efrag-calls-for-balanced-and-costeffective-approach-to-proposed-ghg-protocol-scope-2-changes

**Changes to STC Rebate for Solar Batteries from 1 May 2026**
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-17
Summary: Starting 1 May 2026, amendments to the Renewable Energy (Electricity) Regulations 2001 will reduce the number of small-scale technology certificates (STCs) solar batteries are eligible for. The STC deeming factor will decline more frequently and at a higher rate than under current rules, directly reducing the upfront rebate for new solar battery installations.
Why it matters: Businesses planning solar-plus-storage projects should finalise and lodge STCs before 1 May 2026 — any installation contracted or installed after that date will attract a lower rebate, increasing effective project cost.
Sources: https://www.cer.gov.au/news-and-media/news/2026/february/changes-to-rebate-solar-batteries-1-may
