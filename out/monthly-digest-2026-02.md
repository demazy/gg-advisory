**Signals Digest — 2026-02**

## Energy Transition
**Carbon Abatement Schemes Deliver Emissions Reductions Comparable To Year Road Transport**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The Clean Energy Regulator (CER) reported that emissions reductions from the Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) Scheme and Renewable Energy Target (RET) reached approximately 83.7 million tonnes of CO2-e in 2025, equivalent to removing all cars and trucks from the road for a year. In 2025, Australia added 6.8 GW of renewable energy to the grid, with 4 GW from large-scale power stations and 2.8 GW from small-scale rooftop solar. ACCU issuances hit a record 21.7 million, with expectations of 22 to 26 million in 2026.  
Why it matters: CFOs at companies with significant emissions must prepare for increased ACCU demand as baselines decline under the Safeguard Mechanism.

**Safety Priority Solar Battery Installations Surge**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The CER has conducted 1,000 inspections of solar battery installations under the Cheaper Home Batteries Program, ensuring compliance with safety standards. Starting 1 March 2026, installers must provide geotagged and timestamped photos of critical labelling to enhance safety. Approximately 250,000 households have installed solar batteries since July 2025, contributing around 6 GWh of storage capacity.  
Why it matters: Installers must comply with new photo requirements to avoid delays or rejections of small-scale technology certificate claims.

**Transparency Non Negotiable To Protect Integrity Clean Energy Schemes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The CER emphasized the importance of compliance among participants in Australia’s emissions reduction schemes, warning of potential removal from programs for non-compliance. The latest Quarterly Compliance Update noted actions taken to uphold standards, including the removal of 21 projects from the ACCU Scheme for late reporting. Facilities under the Safeguard Mechanism must comply with baselines by 31 March 2026.  
Why it matters: Boards should ensure compliance with the Safeguard Mechanism to avoid penalties and maintain program participation.

## ESG Reporting
**ISSB Update February 2026**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The ISSB met on 25 February 2026 to discuss amendments to the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards, including a new focus on nature-related disclosures. The ISSB plans to set an effective date for amendments 12–18 months post-issuance, allowing for early application.  
Why it matters: CFOs should prepare for the upcoming changes to sustainability disclosure standards, which will impact reporting practices.

**EFRAG Calls for Balanced and Cost-Effective Approach to Proposed GHG Protocol Scope 2 Changes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-20  
Summary: EFRAG submitted feedback on proposed changes to the GHG Protocol's Scope 2 Guidance, advocating for clarity and proportionality in the revisions. They emphasize the need for a balanced approach that considers the cost-effectiveness of detailed proposals. EFRAG calls for a minimum 120-day consultation period for future changes.  
Why it matters: Companies should monitor developments in GHG Protocol changes as they may affect compliance and reporting requirements.

## Sustainable Finance & Investment
**Annual Report 2025: Investors’ Progress in a Year of Global Turmoil**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-19  
Summary: The IGCC's 2025 Annual Report highlights a year of active climate investment amidst global challenges. It notes the importance of Australia’s role in regional decarbonisation and outlines members' efforts to strengthen net zero investment practices and advocate for necessary policy settings. The report emphasizes the need for systemic corporate engagement to drive emissions reductions.  
Why it matters: Institutional investors must align their strategies with the IGCC's findings to ensure compliance with evolving climate-related investment practices.

**IGCC’s 2030 Strategy: A Clear Plan For This Decade**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-19  
Summary: The IGCC has released its Strategy to 2030, focusing on accelerating the energy transition and addressing physical climate risks. The strategy aims to align investors' fiduciary duties with climate action, emphasizing the role of institutional investors in influencing corporate and public policy decisions.  
Why it matters: Investors should integrate the IGCC’s strategic focus areas into their investment plans to align with long-term climate goals.
