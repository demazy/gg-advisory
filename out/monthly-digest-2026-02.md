## Energy Transition
**Carbon Abatement Schemes Deliver Emissions Reductions Comparable To Year Road Transport**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: In 2025, emissions reductions from the Australian Carbon Credit Unit (ACCU) Scheme and Renewable Energy Target (RET) reached approximately 83.7 million tonnes of CO2-e, equivalent to removing all cars and trucks from the road for a year. The year also saw the addition of 6.8 GW of renewable energy to the grid, with 4 GW from large-scale power stations and 2.8 GW from small-scale rooftop solar. ACCU issuances hit a record 21.7 million, with expectations of 22 to 26 million in 2026.  
Why it matters: CFOs at companies with significant emissions must assess their ACCU strategies ahead of potential supply-demand changes in the carbon market.

**Safety Priority Solar Battery Installations Surge**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The Clean Energy Regulator (CER) has conducted 1,000 inspections of solar battery installations under the Cheaper Home Batteries Program to ensure compliance with safety standards. Starting 1 March 2026, installers must provide geotagged and timestamped photos of critical labelling. Nearly 250,000 households have installed solar batteries, contributing around 6 GWh of storage capacity.  
Why it matters: Installers must adapt to new compliance requirements to avoid delays in small-scale technology certificate claims, impacting project timelines.

**Transparency Non Negotiable To Protect Integrity Clean Energy Schemes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The CER emphasized the importance of compliance in emissions reduction schemes, warning participants to meet obligations or face enforcement actions. The latest compliance update revealed that 21 projects were removed from the ACCU Scheme for failing to report on time. Facilities under the Safeguard Mechanism must comply with baselines by 31 March 2026.  
Why it matters: Boards should ensure compliance with reporting obligations to avoid penalties and safeguard their participation in carbon markets.

## ESG Reporting
**ISSB Update February 2026**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The ISSB met on 25 February 2026 to discuss amendments to the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards, including a new project titled ‘Nature-related Disclosures’. The ISSB plans to set an effective date for amendments 12–18 months after issuance, allowing for early application.  
Why it matters: Australian companies should prepare for upcoming changes in sustainability reporting standards to align with international best practices.

**EFRAG Calls for Balanced and Cost-Effective Approach to Proposed GHG Protocol Scope 2 Changes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-20  
Summary: EFRAG submitted feedback on proposed changes to the GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance, advocating for clarity and proportionality in the updates. EFRAG emphasizes the need for a careful cost-benefit analysis and adequate consultation timelines due to the GHG Protocol's regulatory relevance.  
Why it matters: CFOs should monitor developments in GHG Protocol changes as they may impact compliance costs and reporting requirements.

## Sustainable Finance & Investment
**Sustainable Finance Market to Hit $27 Trillion by 2031**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-22  
Summary: A report by Mordor Intelligence predicts that the sustainable finance market will reach $27 trillion by 2031, with green bonds holding over 53% of the market share in 2025. This growth reflects increasing investor demand for sustainable investment options.  
Why it matters: Institutional investors should reassess their portfolios to capitalize on the expanding sustainable finance market and align with emerging trends.

**'Unbelievable' response to pioneering Aussie Taxonomy green bond**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-05  
Summary: The Australian Taxonomy green bond has received an overwhelming response, indicating strong market interest in sustainable investment vehicles. This pioneering initiative aims to enhance transparency and accountability in green financing.  
Why it matters: Investors should consider participation in green bonds to align with sustainability goals and leverage market momentum.
