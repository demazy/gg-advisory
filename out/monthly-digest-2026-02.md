**Signals Digest — 2026-02**

**Top Lines**
- Carbon abatement schemes reduced emissions by 83.7 million tonnes in 2025.
- New solar battery installation safety requirements take effect on 1 March 2026.
- IFRS 18 requires application from 1 January 2027, impacting financial disclosures.

## Energy Transition
**Carbon Abatement Schemes Deliver Emissions Reductions Comparable To Year Road Transport**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The Clean Energy Regulator (CER) reported that carbon abatement schemes reduced emissions by approximately 83.7 million tonnes of CO2-e in 2025, equivalent to removing all cars and trucks from Australian roads for a year. The report noted a significant increase in renewable energy generation, with 6.8 GW added to the grid, including 4 GW from large-scale power stations and 2.8 GW from rooftop solar. The CER anticipates ACCU issuances of 22 to 26 million in 2026.  
Why it matters: CFOs at companies with significant emissions must prepare for increased ACCU demand under the Safeguard Mechanism, as annual supply may not meet future needs.

**Safety Priority Solar Battery Installations Surge**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The CER has conducted 1,000 inspections of solar battery installations under the Cheaper Home Batteries Program, ensuring compliance with safety standards. Starting 1 March 2026, installers must provide geotagged and timestamped photos of critical labelling. Nearly 250,000 households have installed solar batteries, representing around 6 GWh of capacity. Demand for installations is expected to rise as installation discounts change on 1 May 2026.  
Why it matters: Installers must ensure compliance with new safety requirements to avoid delays or rejections of small-scale technology certificate claims.

**Transparency Non Negotiable To Protect Integrity Clean Energy Schemes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The CER emphasized the importance of compliance within Australia’s emissions reduction schemes, warning participants of potential compliance actions. The latest Quarterly Compliance Update revealed that 21 projects were removed from the ACCU Scheme for failing to report on time. Facilities under the Safeguard Mechanism must avoid exceeding their baseline by the 31 March 2026 compliance deadline.  
Why it matters: Boards should ensure compliance with reporting obligations to avoid penalties and maintain eligibility for carbon credits.

## ESG Reporting
**ISSB Update February 2026**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The ISSB discussed preliminary decisions regarding the IFRS® Sustainability Disclosure Standards, including amendments to the SASB Standards. The effective date for these amendments is set for 12–18 months post-issuance, with early application permitted. The ISSB aims to enhance alignment with climate-related content in the SASB Standards.  
Why it matters: CFOs should prepare for changes in sustainability reporting standards that may impact financial disclosures starting in 2027.

**EFRAG Calls for Balanced and Cost-Effective Approach to Proposed GHG Protocol Scope 2 Changes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-20  
Summary: EFRAG submitted feedback on proposed changes to the GHG Protocol's Scope 2 Guidance, advocating for clarity and proportionality in the guidance. EFRAG emphasizes the need for a balanced approach to ensure that changes do not impose excessive costs on businesses while improving comparability.  
Why it matters: Companies must monitor developments in GHG Protocol changes, as they could affect compliance and reporting requirements in Australia.

## Sustainable Finance & Investment
**Annual Report 2025: Investors’ Progress in a Year of Global Turmoil**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-19  
Summary: The IGCC's 2025 Annual Report highlights a year of significant climate policy activity in Australia, including the rollout of mandatory climate-related disclosures. The report emphasizes the need for investors to strengthen net zero investment practices and engage with policymakers to support an orderly transition.  
Why it matters: Institutional investors must align their strategies with emerging climate policies to mitigate risks and capitalize on opportunities in the transition to a net zero economy.

**IGCC’s 2030 Strategy: A Clear Plan For This Decade**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-19  
Summary: The IGCC has released its Strategy to 2030, focusing on accelerating the energy transition and addressing physical climate risks. The strategy aims to align investors' fiduciary duties with climate action, emphasizing the importance of systemic engagement and investment in climate solutions.  
Why it matters: Investors should integrate the IGCC's strategic priorities into their investment frameworks to enhance resilience and support the transition to a sustainable economy.
