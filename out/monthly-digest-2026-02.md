## Energy Transition
**Energy security: Keeping the lights on through the energy transition (QLD)**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-26  
Summary: Rainer Korte, Commissioner at the Australian Energy Market Commission (AEMC), addressed energy security challenges amid Australia's energy transition. The National Electricity Market (NEM) is projected to see electricity consumption nearly double by 2050, necessitating a fivefold increase in grid-scale wind and solar, an 18-fold increase in storage capacity, and an additional 6,000 km of transmission. The annualised capital cost for this transition is estimated at $128 billion.  
Why it matters: CFOs at energy-intensive companies should assess their exposure to rising electricity demand and consider investments in renewable energy sources to mitigate future costs.

**Energy security: Keeping the lights on through the energy transition (NSW)**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-24  
Summary: In a similar address in NSW, Korte highlighted the need for reliable energy sources as coal plants retire. The AEMC's Transition Plan for System Security identifies gaps that require coordinated action over the next decade. Origin Energy has extended the operation of Eraring Power Station to 30 April 2029, reducing system security risks.  
Why it matters: Boards should review their energy procurement strategies in light of potential coal retirements and the need for reliable backup generation.

**Carbon Abatement Schemes Deliver Emissions Reductions Comparable To Year Road Transport**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The Clean Energy Regulator reported that carbon abatement schemes reduced emissions by approximately 83.7 million tonnes CO2-e in 2025, equivalent to removing all cars and trucks from the road for a year. The Cheaper Home Batteries Program led to the installation of over 193,000 batteries, providing 4.6 GWh of capacity.  
Why it matters: Investors in renewable energy projects should track the performance of carbon credit schemes as demand for Australian Carbon Credit Units (ACCUs) is expected to grow.

## ESG Reporting
**ISSB Update February 2026**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-01  
Summary: The International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB) met to discuss updates on sustainability disclosure standards. Key decisions included setting an effective date for amendments 12–18 months post-issuance and permitting early application. The ISSB is also enhancing the SASB Standards to align with climate-related content.  
Why it matters: CFOs should prepare for the upcoming changes in sustainability reporting standards, which may affect compliance and disclosure practices.

**EFRAG Calls for Balanced and Cost-Effective Approach to Proposed GHG Protocol Scope 2 Changes**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-20  
Summary: EFRAG submitted feedback on proposed changes to the GHG Protocol's Scope 2 Guidance, advocating for clarity and cost-effectiveness. They emphasized the need for a balanced approach to ensure that changes do not impose excessive complexity on businesses.  
Why it matters: Companies should evaluate their current emissions reporting practices and prepare for potential adjustments in compliance requirements.

## Sustainable Finance & Investment
**Statement on Monetary Policy**  
PUBLISHED: 2026-02-03  
Summary: The Reserve Bank of Australia released its Statement on Monetary Policy, assessing economic conditions and risks. The document will inform the Board's monetary policy decisions, which aim to maintain price stability and full employment.  
Why it matters: CFOs should consider the implications of monetary policy on financing costs and investment strategies in the current economic climate.

**Future Made in Australia (FMIA) Innovation Fund — ARENA**
Administrator: Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA)
Funding: $1.5 billion total pool; no per-project maximum (min 50% co-contribution)
Deadline: Rolling — no fixed close date (launched December 2025)
Stage: Demonstration to commercialisation (TRL 3+)
Summary: ARENA's flagship $1.5 billion fund targets three priority streams: Green Metals ($750M for iron, steel, alumina, aluminium), Renewable Energy Technology Manufacturing ($200M for batteries, solar components, electrolysers), and Low Carbon Liquid Fuels ($250M for sustainable aviation fuel and renewable diesel). Minimum 50% co-contribution required. Grants above $50M require ministerial sign-off. Submit via Expression of Interest in ARENANet.
Why it matters: The largest single cleantech manufacturing opportunity in Australia's history. ARENA encourages early engagement before submitting an EOI — contact their team first.
Sources: https://arena.gov.au/funding/future-made-in-australia-innovation-fund/

Administrator: Department of Industry, Science and Resources / business.gov.au
Funding: $50,000–$5.25M matched grants (two tiers)
Deadline: Rolling — ~90% of initial pool projected spent by June 2026
Stage: Early-stage commercialisation to growth
Summary: The federal government's primary SME commercialisation program offers matched grants aligned with National Reconstruction Fund priorities including renewables, low-emissions technologies, and critical minerals.
